=== Role ===
Senior Equity Research Analyst.

=== Task ===
I need you to score the "Capital Allocation Skill" of company {company_name} on a qualitative scale of 0 to 4 points.
Note: 6 out of 10 points for Capital Allocation Skill are already determined by leverage and share count behavior.
Your role is to allocate the remaining 0–4 points only based on evidence of intentional discipline or clear qualitative
failures not already captured in the quantitative score.
Scores of 3 or 4 should be assigned only in exceptional cases with explicit, concrete evidence.
If evidence is ambiguous or generic, default to the lower score.

=== Input data ===
1. Financial Context (Hard Data)
Share Count Trend (5yr CAGR): {share_cagr_percentage}%
Balance Sheet Safety (Net Debt/EBITDA): {leverage_ratio}x


2. Qualitative Text Data
    A. Business Description Snippet:
        "{business_description}"
    B. Risk Factors
        "{risk_factors}"
    C. MD&A
        "{mda}"

=== Instructions ===
1.Analyze the text above in the context of the Hard Data and assign a score for the following two categories.
  Category 1: M&A Discipline (0-2 Points)
    Score 2: One of the following clearly applies:
        - The text indicates a focus on organic growth OR disciplined "bolt-on" acquisitions. No red flags in Risk Factors about failed integrations or goodwill impairments.
        - Disciplined serial acquirer with good integration track record, limited impairments, and deals that appear accretive over time
    Score 1: The company is a "Serial Acquirer" / Roll-up, but risks seem managed.
    Score 0: Risk factors mention specific struggles to integrate past acquisitions, significant impairment charges, or a reliance on M&A to mask slowing organic growth.
  Category 2: Capital Strategy Intent (0-2 Points)
    Score 2 (The Outsider): Management explicitly articulates a framework (e.g., "We compare buybacks to intrinsic value," or "Hurdle rate for CAPEX is X%"). The strategy matches the data (e.g., they say they return capital, and the share count is actually shrinking).
            OR
            The language is more conventional BUT the capital behavior has been exceptionally disciplined for ≥7–10 years AND strongly aligns with owner-oriented outcomes (very low/negative dilution, intelligent use of leverage, avoidance of value-destructive moves)
    Score 1 (Standard): Generic language ("We use cash for general corporate purposes").
    Score 0 (Contradictory/Poor): Management claims to be shareholder-friendly but is diluting shares heavily (see Context), or they are issuing debt/equity to fund dividends.
2. Add the 2 scores from above. The result will be the total score part of the output.

AUTOMATIC PENALTIES:
- If acquisitions are primarily funded with stock, Category 1 cannot exceed 1 point.
- If goodwill or intangibles represent a dominant portion of past acquisitions, Category 1 cannot exceed 1 point.
- If Risk Factors mention integration risk, impairment risk, or acquisition dependency, Category 1 must be reduced by at least 1 point.

NOTE FOR CATEGORY 2:
Generic language such as “capital allocation,” “returning value to shareholders,” or “general corporate purposes”
does NOT qualify for 2 points.
To award 2 points, the company must explicitly describe a decision framework
(e.g., hurdle rates, intrinsic value comparisons, or a stated order of capital allocation priorities)
AND this framework must align with the provided share count and leverage data.
If alignment is partial or unclear, award at most 1 point.

TIE-BREAKING RULE:
When deciding between two adjacent scores, always choose the lower score unless the higher score
is clearly supported by multiple, specific pieces of evidence from the text.

EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT:
If assigning 2 points in any category, cite at least one specific phrase or policy from the provided text
that directly supports the score.

=== Output Format ===
Your analysis must contain:
**Total Score:** [Score]/4
**Analysis:** [2 or 3 sentences explaining the decision for the score.]

{format}
