=== Role ===
Senior Equity Research Analyst.

=== Task ===
I need you to score the "Growth Curve / Inflection Point" of company {company_name} on a qualitative scale of 0 to 5 points.
In the context of "100 Baggers," we are looking for the "S-Curve" sweet spot: a company that is not just growing, but
where growth is *accelerating* due to structural reasons (new products, market adoption), not temporary factors.

=== Input data ===
1. Financial Context (Hard Data)
    Historical Baseline (Revenue CAGR): {revenue_cagr}%
    Current Momentum (Last 2 Quarters Avg YoY Growth): {recent_revenue_growth}%
    Acceleration Delta (Momentum - Baseline): {acceleration_delta} percentage points

2. Qualitative Text Data
    A. MD&A (Results of Operations - Revenue Drivers):
        "{mda}"

=== Instructions ===
Analyze the text above in the context of the Hard Data and assign a score for the following two categories.

Category 1: The Growth Trajectory (0-3 Points)
    Score 3 (Hyper-Growth/Accelerating):
        - "Current Momentum" is high (>20%) AND significantly higher than the "Historical Baseline" (Positive Delta).
        - OR the company is maintaining hyper-growth (>30%) for multiple years.
    Score 2 (Steady/Mature):
        - Growth is consistent but not exploding (e.g., 10-20%). "Acceleration Delta" is near zero. The company is in a
        predictable "earning power" phase.
    Score 1 (Stagnant/Low Growth):
        - Growth is low (<5%) or flat.
    Score 0 (Declining):
        - Revenue is shrinking (Negative growth).

Category 2: Quality & Sustainability of Growth (0-2 Points)
    Score 2 (Structural/Organic):
        - The text confirms growth is driven by VOLUME (selling more units), NEW PRODUCTS, or NEW CUSTOMERS.
        - Backlog or "Remaining Performance Obligations" (RPO) are growing fast (leading indicator).
    Score 1 (Mixed):
        - Growth is partly driven by price increases or favorable FX (currency) rather than volume.
        - Growth is decent, but Risk Factors suggest it might be cyclical (e.g., a commodity cycle upswing).
    Score 0 (Low Quality/Artificial):
        - The "Acceleration" in the hard data is explicitly attributed to a recent ACQUISITION (inorganic).
        - Growth is driven by a one-time event (e.g., a single large contract or regulatory change) that won't repeat.

2. Add the 2 scores from above. The result will be the total score (0-5).

AUTOMATIC PENALTIES:
- If the MD&A explicitly states that revenue growth was "primarily due to the acquisition of [Company X]," deduct 1 point
    from the Total Score (unless the organic growth component remains >15%).
- If "Current Momentum" is negative (Revenue is shrinking), the Total Score must be 0, regardless of the text.

TIE-BREAKING RULE:
If the "Acceleration Delta" is positive (Recent growth > Historical trend), lean toward the higher score. If the Delta
is negative (Growth is slowing), lean toward the lower score.

EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT:
You must quote the specific phrase from the MD&A that explains the *source* of the growth (e.g., "driven by 20% increase
in volume" vs "driven by price mix").

=== Output Format ===
Your analysis must contain:
**Total Score:** [Score]/5
**Analysis:** [2 sentences explaining if the company is at an inflection point or just steady state. Mention if growth is organic or acquired.]

{format}
