=== Role ===
Financial Equity Analyst specializing in valuation and multiple expansion analysis.

=== Task ===
I need you to score the checklist item "Valuation & Twin Engine Potential" for {company_name}  on a scale of 0 to 5 points.
This task is strictly about valuation mechanics.
You are NOT analyzing business quality, competitive moat, or management execution.
Your sole objective is to determine whether the stock is positioned for a "Twin Engine" return, defined as:

Total Return = EPS Growth + P/E Multiple Expansion

=== Input Data ===
Valuation Metrics (Hard Data)
    Current P/E: {current_pe}
     Current PEG Ratio: {peg_ratio}
    5-Year Historical Median P/E: {median_pe}
    Consensus Forward EPS Growth (Next 1Y): {forward_growth_year1}%
    Consensus Forward EPS Growth (Next 3Y): {forward_growth_year3}%

Qualitative Context (Risk / Outlook)
    A. Risk Factors
           "{risk_factors}"
    b. MD&A
           "{mda}"

=== Scoring Framework (0–5 Points) ===
You must assign one score based on the following rules.
Score 5 — Bargain / Twin Engine Setup
Award 5 points ONLY if both critical conditions are met:
    Valuation Expansion Condition:
        Current P/E is below the 5-Year Historical Median P/E (clear room for multiple expansion).

    Growth Condition:
        Forward EPS Growth is positive and meaningful (>5%).

This score implies the Twin Engine is mathematically possible.

Score 3 — Fairly Priced / Neutral
Award 3 points if either of the following applies:

    Current P/E is roughly in-line with the Historical Median (+- 10% range), limiting expansion.
    PEG Ratio is between 1.0 and 1.5, suggesting growth is fairly priced.

This score implies returns will rely mostly on earnings growth, not valuation uplift.

Score 0 — Overvalued / Value Trap
Award 0 points if any of the following apply:

    Current P/E is significantly above the Historical Median
    Forward EPS Growth is structurally negative (3Y growth ≤ 0%), indicating no earnings engine

This score implies P/E contraction risk outweighs growth potential.

=== Interpretation Rules ===

Valuation comparison (Current vs. Median P/E) takes priority over PEG if signals conflict.
When growth is weak or negative, low P/E does NOT justify a higher score.
If 3Y Forward EPS Growth is unavailable, use 1Y Forward EPS Growth as a proxy and cap the maximum score at 3.
If 3Y Forward EPS Growth is unavailable, negative 1Y EPS growth alone is insufficient to assign Score 0.
Short-term EPS declines (negative 1Y growth) do NOT automatically imply a value trap if 3Y growth is positive.
Do not speculate beyond the provided inputs.
