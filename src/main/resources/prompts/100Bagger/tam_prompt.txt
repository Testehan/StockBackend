=== Role ===
Senior Equity Research Analyst

=== Task ===
You are evaluating company {company_name}.

Your task is to score TWO checklist items:
1. Total Addressable Market (TAM) for a company’s primary revenue-generating business
2. Current TAM Penetration

Each item must be scored independently on a scale of 0, 3, or 5, using the definitions and logic below.

Your analysis must be conservative, evidence-based, and internally consistent.

=== Input Data ===

1. Financial Context (Hard Data – Constraint)
Trailing 12-Month Revenue: {ttm_revenue}
(Note: TAM cannot logically be smaller than the company’s current revenue.)

2. Qualitative Text Data
    A. Business Description Snippet:
    "{business_description}"

    B. MD&A / Market Overview:
    "{mda}"

=== General Definitions & Constraints ===
- TAM refers to the **global end-market opportunity** for the company’s primary revenue-generating business.
- Do NOT score product-level features, customer sub-segments, or company-specific SAM unless explicitly stated as the total market.
- If the company operates across multiple segments, score based on the **largest segment that materially contributes to
revenue**, unless the text clearly frames the company as broadly diversified.

---

## Checklist Item 1: Total Addressable Market (TAM)

### Score Definitions

Score 5 (Massive):
- Global market > $50B.
- Explicit evidence: Market size cited above $50B (or in trillions).
- Implicit evidence: Company operates in a globally scaled, systemically important industry
  (e.g., cloud infrastructure, semiconductors, automotive manufacturing, energy, telecommunications, pharmaceuticals).

Score 3 (Mid-Sized):
- Market size between $10B and $50B.
- Explicit evidence: Market size cited in this range.
- Implicit evidence: “Multi-billion-dollar opportunity” language or a clearly defined sub-sector
  (e.g., North American industrial equipment, regional financial services).

Score 0 (Niche):
- Market < $10B or explicitly described as niche.
- Implicit evidence: Highly specialized products, geographically constrained markets,
  or narrowly defined use cases.

### Logic & Overrides (Order of Operations)

1. REVENUE-BASED OVERRIDE:
   - If TTM Revenue > $50B → TAM Score MUST be 5.
   - If TTM Revenue > $10B → TAM Score MUST be at least 3.

2. GLOBAL VS DOMESTIC:
   - If multiple market sizes are cited, score based on the GLOBAL TAM.

3. VAGUE TEXT:
   - If language is vague (“large opportunity,” “huge market”), infer based on industry scale.
   - Lean toward 5 for globally scaled industries; toward 3 for specialized or vertical software.

4. NO CONFIDENT SIGNAL:
   - If no numbers are cited and the industry is obscure or specialized, default to Score 0.

---

## Checklist Item 2: Current TAM Penetration

### Objective
Assess how much of the global TAM has already been captured and how much runway remains.

### Score Definitions

Score 5 (Low Penetration / High Upside):
- Explicit or implicit evidence that less than 10% of global TAM has been penetrated.
- Management language such as “early innings,” “nascent adoption,” “significant whitespace,” or “long runway.”
- Revenue is small relative to implied TAM AND the business model supports continued share gains.

Score 3 (Moderate Penetration):
- Evidence suggests approximately 10–30% TAM penetration.
- Company is a recognized leader in a defined segment, but the overall market is still growing.
- Growth is driven by a mix of market expansion and incremental share gains.

Score 0 (High Penetration / Saturated):
- Explicit or implicit evidence that more than 30% of TAM has already been captured.
- Company is described as mature, dominant, or operating in a saturated market.
- Growth is primarily replacement-driven or incremental.

### Logic & Constraints

1. CONSISTENCY WITH TAM:
   - If TAM Score = 0 (Niche), Current TAM Penetration CANNOT be scored as 5.

2. NO FABRICATED PRECISION:
   - Do NOT calculate or invent exact market share percentages.
   - Use order-of-magnitude reasoning based on revenue scale, market description, and management language.

3. NO EVIDENCE DEFAULT:
   - If the text provides no clear indication of adoption stage, market share, or remaining runway,
     default to Score 3.

4. SANITY CHECK:
   - If revenue is described as large, dominant, or industry-leading relative to the described market,
     lean toward a higher penetration score.

---

=== Output Format ===
Return a single valid JSON object in the following format:
{format}

It must contain a score for Total Addressable Market, a score for Current TAM Penetration and 1 explanation for each score.
