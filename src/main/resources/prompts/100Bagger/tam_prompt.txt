=== Role ===
Senior Equity Research Analyst

=== Task ===
I need you to score the "Total Addressable Market (TAM)" for a company’s primary revenue-generating business,
 {company_name} on a scale of 0, 3, or 5.
Your goal is to determine if the global market opportunity is Niche (<$10B), Mid-Sized ($10–50B), or Massive (>$50B).

=== Input Data ===
1. Financial Context (Hard Data - The Reality Check)
Trailing 12-Month Revenue: {ttm_revenue}
(Note: The TAM cannot logically be smaller than the company's current revenue.)

2. Qualitative Text Data
    A. Business Description Snippet:
        "{business_description}"
    B. MD&A / Market Overview:
        "{mda}"

=== Instructions ===
Analyze the text provided combined with the Hard Data to assign one of the following scores.

SCORE DEFINITIONS:
Score 5 (Massive): Global Market > $50 Billion.
    - Explicit evidence: The text cites a market size figure greater than $50B (or in Trillions).
    - Implicit evidence: The company operates broadly in a massive sector (“Globally scaled, capital-intensive, or
    systemically important industries (e.g., cloud infrastructure, semiconductors, automotive manufacturing, energy,
    telecommunications, pharmaceuticals)”) where the market is universally known to be >$50B, even if no specific number
    is cited.

Score 3 (Mid-Sized): Market $10 Billion – $50 Billion.
    - Explicit evidence: Text cites a market size between $10B and $50B.
    - Implicit evidence: Text describes a "multi-billion dollar opportunity" without specifying a number, or defines a
    specific sub-sector (e.g., "North American HVAC equipment").

Score 0 (Niche): Market < $10 Billion or "Niche".
    - Explicit evidence: Text cites a market size below $10B.
    - Implicit evidence: The company describes itself as a "niche" provider, or the market is geographically limited
    (e.g., "regional bank," "local utility") or highly specialized (e.g., "equipment for watch repair").

LOGIC & OVERRIDES (Order of Operations):
1. THE REVENUE FLOOR:
   - If TTM Revenue is > $50B, the Score MUST be 5 (Override any text analysis).
   - If TTM Revenue is > $10B, the Score MUST be at least 3.
2. GLOBAL VS. DOMESTIC:
   - If the text gives two numbers (e.g., "Domestic SAM: $8B" vs "Global TAM: $60B"), score based on the GLOBAL figure.
3. VAGUE TEXT:
   - If the text uses vague terms like "huge opportunity" or "massive market" without numbers, look at the industry.
   If it is a commodity or major global industry, lean toward Score 5. If it is a specialized product, lean toward Score 3.
4. CONFIDENCE:
   - If the text mentions NO numbers and the industry is obscure, default to Score 0 (Niche) to be conservative.

Multi-Segment Rule:
If the company operates in multiple segments, score the TAM based on the largest segment that materially contributes to
revenue, unless the text explicitly frames the company as diversified across several unrelated markets.

RECENCY & TIME-SENSITIVITY (2025–2026 Context):
Market size figures should preferably come from sources dated 2023 or later.
If the only available figure is older than 2023, apply a reasonable growth uplift for high-growth sectors (AI, cloud,
semiconductors, EVs, renewables, digital health, fintech infrastructure) of +15–40% depending on sector momentum, and
document the adjustment.
For mature/defensive sectors (traditional retail, legacy manufacturing, regional services) do NOT apply growth uplift
unless explicitly supported.

SAM vs TAM CLARIFICATION:
Always prefer GLOBAL TAM when available.
If only SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market) is provided and no global TAM is mentioned, downgrade one level unless the
business is clearly global by nature (e.g. SaaS, semiconductors, cloud infrastructure).
Example: "North America SAM $12B" with no global figure → treat as Score 3 at best, even if implicit global potential exists.

DEFAULT WHEN EVIDENCE IS THIN (Updated conservative rule):
If NO market size numbers appear anywhere in the text AND the industry is not obviously obscure/niche → default to Score
3 (Mid-Sized) rather than 0, especially for technology, healthcare, or industrial sectors founded after 2010.
Reserve Score 0 default only for clearly specialized, regional, or hobby/professional-niche businesses (examples: luxury
watch repair tools, artisanal food ingredients, local/regional waste management, boutique consulting).

HIGH-GROWTH & EMERGING SECTORS OVERRIDE (AI, Quantum, Web3, Climate Tech, Autonomous Systems, etc.):
When the company is in a currently hyper-growing category (2024–2026), and credible third-party forecasts (Gartner, IDC,
McKinsey, BloombergNEF, ARK Invest, etc.) commonly place the 2030 TAM >$100B–$1T+, you may assign Score 5 even if current
cited TAM is in the $20–60B range, provided the text shows the company is participating in the core value chain of that expansion.
Document the reasoning and name at least one reputable source family that supports the >$50B long-term view.

=== Output Format ===
Your analysis must contain:
**Total Score:** [Score]/5
**Analysis:** [2 or 3 sentences explaining the decision for the score.]
{format}
