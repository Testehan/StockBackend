=== Role ===
Senior Equity Research Analyst.

=== Task ===
I need you to score the "Scalability of Business" of company {company_name} on a scale of 0 to 5 points.
Scalability is defined by the ability to increase revenue with minimal incremental costs (Operating Leverage) and
minimal capital investment (Capital Efficiency).
Your goal is to distinguish between "Linear Growth" (e.g., Consulting, Heavy Industry) and "Exponential/Non-Linear
Growth" (e.g., Software, IP Licensing).

=== Input data ===
1. Financial Context (Hard Data)
    Gross Margin (Avg): {avg_gross_profit_margin}%
    Capex Intensity (Capex/Revenue Avg): {avg_capex_intensity}%
    Asset Efficiency Trend:
        Revenue CAGR: {revenue_cagr}%
        Total Assets CAGR: {total_assets_cagr}%

2. Qualitative Text Data
    A. Business Description Snippet:
        "{business_description}"

=== Instructions ===
Analyze the text above in the context of the Hard Data and assign a score for the following two categories.

Category 1: Business Model Physics (0-3 Points)
    Score 3 (Digital/IP/Network): The core product is Software, Intellectual Property, Licensing, or a Platform with
            strong network effects. Marginal costs are near zero. Gross Margins are typically High (>70%).
    Score 2 (Replicable/Brand): The business requires physical assets but has a "cookie-cutter" expansion model
            (e.g., Franchising, Scalable Retail) or high-margin manufacturing with strong pricing power. Gross Margins
            are Moderate (40-70%).
    Score 1 (Linear Service/Light Industry): Growth requires hiring linear labor (e.g., Consulting, Logistics) or
            standard manufacturing.
    Score 0 (Heavy/Commodity): Heavy Industry, Utilities, or pure Commodity. Growth requires massive ongoing capital or
            linear raw material/labor input. Gross Margins are typically Low (<30%).

Category 2: Evidence of Operating Leverage (0-2 Points)
    Score 2 (Proven Scale): The "Asset Efficiency Trend" explicitly shows Revenue CAGR is significantly higher than Total
            Assets CAGR. Capex Intensity is low (<5%).
    Score 1 (Neutral/Mixed): Revenue and Assets are growing at roughly the same rate. Capex is moderate (5-10%).
    Score 0 (Negative Scale/Bloat): Total Assets are growing faster than Revenue (diminishing returns), OR Capex Intensity
            is High (>10%).

2. Add the 2 scores from above. The result will be the total score (0-5).

AUTOMATIC PENALTIES:
- If the Business Model is "Consulting" or "Professional Services" (where Revenue is tied directly to Headcount),
Category 1 cannot exceed 1 point, even if Capex is low.

TIE-BREAKING RULE:
When in doubt between two scores, look at the "Asset Efficiency Trend." If Assets are growing faster than Revenue,
always choose the lower score.

EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT:
Cite the specific financial metric (from the Input Data) or the specific text snippet that justified your categorization.

=== Output Format ===
Your analysis must contain:
**Total Score:** [Score]/5
**Analysis:** [2 or 3 sentences explaining the decision. Explicitly mention if the Asset Trend supported or hurt the score.]

{format}
